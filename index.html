How to get there
Our wedding will take place in La Turballe, a small seaside town in north-western France. Getting there takes a little planning, especially for those travelling from abroad, but we hope the information below will help make your journey as smooth as possible.
Flying from Finland Helsinki ➟ Paris (CDG airport) direct flight - approx. 2h30–3h
Helsinki ➟ Nantes One layover – approx. 5h-7h
From Paris The easiest way to reach Saint-Nazaire from Paris is by train. Trains leave regularly from Montparnasse railway station (70 minutes form CDG airport), either direct to Saint-Nazaire, or with a change in Nantes.
Paris Montparnasse ➟ Nantes approx. 2h
Paris Montparnasse ➟ Saint-Nazaire approx. 3h
Some trains leave directly from CDG airport, but are usually longer and more expensive.
For train routes, schedule and tickets, visit: www.sncf-connect.com
If you wish to rent a car in Paris, the drive to Saint-Nazaire takes about 5 hours.
From Nantes Trains leave every hour from Nantes railway station to Saint-Nazaire and the journey takes about 40 minutes.
The car ride takes about 60 minutes.

Visit the area
Our wedding will take place in La Turballe, in the Presqu’île de Guérande, a beautiful and diverse region that is well worth visiting. Whether you are traveling as a couple, with family, or with friends, you will find a wide variety of things to do and see, from nature and seaside landscapes to cultural visits and city outings.
Presqu’île de Guérande Right around La Turballe, the Presqu’île de Guérande offers a wonderful mix of coastline, heritage, and local traditions. It is a perfect area for relaxed walks, enjoying the ocean, and discovering the character of the region.
You can enjoy: ✦ Beaches and coastal paths along the Atlantic ✦ Charming and pitoresque seaside towns ✦ The breathtaking salt marshes ✦ The medieval walled town of Guérande
More on the tourist office of La Baule - Guérande website:
labaule-guerande.com
Saint-Nazaire About half an hour away, Saint-Nazaire offers a different atmosphere, closely linked to the sea and its industrial and maritime past. The city is known for its major shipyards and its role in transatlantic ocean liner history. It combines cultural visits with coastal scenery, making it a great option for a half-day or day trip.
Highlights include the submarine base area, maritime heritage sites, and pleasant seafront walks .
More on the tourist office of Saint-Nazaire website:
www.saint-nazaire-tourisme.uk
Nantes Located just over an hour from La Turballe, Nantes is a vibrant and creative city, perfect for a full day of exploration. Its historic center, cultural life, and lively neighborhoods offer a nice contrast with the coast. Visitors can combine history, museums, shopping, and restaurants in one visit.
Among the city’s best-known attractions are the Château des Ducs de Bretagne and Les Machines de l’Île, along with many other cultural and architectural discoveries throughout the city.
More on the tourist office of Nantes website:
www.levoyageanantes.fr
